## Report Writing Principles

### 1. Organize by Insight, Not by Source

**Bad** (organized by persona):

```
## What the Historian Found
[Historian findings]

## What the Contrarian Found
[Contrarian findings]
```

**Good** (organized by theme):

```
## Historical Evolution and Future Trajectory

Historical context (Historian, Archaeologist):
[Synthesized findings]

Critical perspective (Contrarian):
[Challenges to the narrative]

Future outlook (Futurist):
[Where it's heading]
```

### 2. Highlight Contradictions

Don't smooth over disagreements. When personas contradict each other:

```
**Consensus view**: [What most agree on]

**Contrarian challenge**: The Contrarian found evidence that [contradiction]

**Resolution**: [Which is better supported, or "Both may be true in different contexts"]
```

### 3. Use Evidence Hierarchy

Prioritize findings by evidence quality:

1. **Primary sources, multiple personas agree** -> High confidence
2. **Secondary sources, multiple personas** -> Medium-high confidence
3. **Primary source, single persona** -> Medium confidence
4. **Secondary source, single persona** -> Medium-low confidence
5. **Speculative, single persona** -> Low confidence, flag as speculative

### 4. Preserve Uncertainty

When things are unknown, say so:

```
**Current evidence is insufficient to determine** whether [question].

The Historian found [one view], while the Futurist suggests [another view], but neither has strong primary evidence.

**Research needed**: [What would resolve this]
```

### 5. Focus on Strategic Implications

The goal is actionable intelligence, not just information:

- **Not just**: "AI adoption is growing"
- **Better**: "AI adoption is growing, which will create second-order effects on [stakeholder], requiring [action] by [timeframe]"

### 6. Be Information-Dense

Every sentence should add value:

- **Weak**: "The research revealed many interesting findings about the subject."
- **Strong**: "Three critical adoption barriers emerged: technical complexity, regulatory uncertainty, and misaligned incentives."

### 7. Synthesize, Don't Summarize

- **Summary**: Reports what was found
- **Synthesis**: Integrates findings to create new understanding

Aim for synthesis that generates insights not present in any single persona finding.

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing the report, verify:

- [ ] Executive synthesis is information-dense (not just summary)
- [ ] Organized by theme/insight, not by persona
- [ ] Contradictions preserved and analyzed (not smoothed)
- [ ] Evidence quality clearly indicated
- [ ] Strategic implications address second/third-order effects
- [ ] Research gaps explicitly documented
- [ ] Novel hypotheses generated from synthesis
- [ ] Temporal analysis covers past/present/future
- [ ] Cross-domain insights included
- [ ] All major findings supported by citations
- [ ] Confidence levels honest and accurate
- [ ] Actionable recommendations provided
- [ ] Limitations acknowledged
- [ ] Bottom line clearly stated

When time/space is limited, prioritize sections:

**Must Have**:

- Executive Synthesis

- Strategic Implications
- Research Gaps

**Should Have**:

- Evidence Portfolio
- Temporal Analysis
- Novel Hypotheses
- Recommendations

**Nice to Have**:

- Cross-Domain Insights (can integrate into themes)
- Detailed methodological notes
- Complete appendices
